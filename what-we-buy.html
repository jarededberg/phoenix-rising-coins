What We Buy

Copper, silver, gold, older nickels, and old paper currency — or you're just not sure what it is — we want to see it. Here's a closer look at what we're most often buying.

MetalWhat we look forExamples
Copper U.S. copper coinage in any condition, especially older and low-mintage dates Wheat pennies, Indian Head cents, large cents, early Lincoln cents
Silver Any pre-1965 U.S. silver coinage or recognized silver dollars, plus silver bullion pieces Morgan & Peace dollars, Franklin/Kennedy halves, silver dimes & quarters, "junk silver" bags
Gold U.S. and foreign gold coinage, any era or condition Gold eagles, older gold dollars/quarter eagles, gold commemoratives, foreign gold coins
Nickels Older and collectible five-cent pieces, including the silver war nickels Buffalo/Indian Head nickels, Liberty Head ("V") nickels, 1942–1945 silver war nickels (35% silver)
Currency Old and obsolete U.S. paper money, especially notes backed by silver or gold Silver certificates, gold certificates, red seal legal tender notes, national bank notes, large-size bills
Other Full or partial collections, mixed jars of change, foreign or unidentified coins Inherited collections, estate coin boxes, "not sure what this is" coins
Condition & quantity

Any condition. Any amount.

From a single worn silver dime to a full estate collection, we evaluate on the same basis: metal content, rarity, and collector demand. Heavily worn or damaged coins can still have value — let us take a look before you assume otherwise.
